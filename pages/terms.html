Platform Terms of Service

Governing the use of Pixel Phantoms for Events, Hackathons, and Community Interactions.

Last Updated:

1. Platform Services & Scope

Pixel Phantoms provides a digital platform ("Service") for:

  • Event Hosting: Organizing workshops, seminars, and technical meetups.
  • Hackathon Management: Facilitating team registration, project submission, and evaluation.
  • Community Interaction: Leaderboards, mentorship, and peer reviews.

We reserve the right to modify, suspend, or discontinue any aspect of the Platform Services at any time without notice.

2. Hackathon & Event Participation

By registering for an event or hackathon via our Platform, you agree to:

  • Accuracy: Provide true and accurate registration information.
  • Fair Play: Adhere to specific event rulebooks. Plagiarism, cheating, or sabotage will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Team Conduct: Treat teammates and competitors with respect. Toxic behavior during events is grounds for a permanent ban.

3. Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)

We respect the creativity of our students. Our policy regarding Hackathon Submissions is as follows:

  • Student Ownership: You retain full ownership of any code, design, or project you create and submit to our hackathons.
  • License to Showcase: By submitting a project, you grant Pixel Phantoms a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to display, demo, and promote your project on our website and social media for community purposes.
  • Open Source: We encourage, but do not mandate, open-sourcing your submissions under licenses like MIT or Apache 2.0.

4. User Conduct & Compliance

Users must not use the Platform to host, display, or share content that is:

  • Defamatory, obscene, or invasive of another's privacy.
  • In violation of any Indian laws or regulations (including the IT Act, 2000).
  • Malicious software, viruses, or code designed to disrupt the Platform.

Pixel Phantoms is a student-run community. We are not liable for:

  • Platform downtime during critical submission windows (though we try our best!).
  • Loss of data or project files. Always backup your hackathon submissions locally.
